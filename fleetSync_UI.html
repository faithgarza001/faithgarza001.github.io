Overview

The client, fleet fueling managers, faced challenges with their existing monolithic BI application, which was outdated and inefficient. Our team was tasked with modernizing their system into a scalable SaaS solution that would improve user experience and operational efficiency.

  • Conducting stakeholder interviews and user surveys to gather insights
  • Developing user personas and journey maps to guide design decisions
  • Designing wireframes, interactive prototypes, and final UI components
  • Facilitating usability testing sessions to refine the design
  • Implementing responsive design principles for mobile compatibility
  • Creating comprehensive design system and component library

Goal

The objective was to address the limitations of a monolithic BI application by designing a scalable SaaS platform that improved user engagement and operational efficiency. Specifically, the project aimed to:

  • Increase user satisfaction by 40%, measured through post-launch surveys
  • Enhance collaboration with features like shared reports and annotations
  • Improve navigation efficiency, reducing task completion times by 25%
  • Enable seamless scalability to support a 300% increase in user base without performance issues
  • Reduce onboarding time for new users by implementing intuitive workflows
  • Implement modern accessibility standards for inclusive design

Interviews

During the ideation phase of the project, I conducted user interviews to build new personas and to inform the design. Together with the team, we prepared an interview script with 32 open-ended questions, focusing on our target audiences' values, motivations, and daily routines. In 4 days, I recruited and interviewed 7 users remotely. We referenced the user interview findings throughout the entire design process.

  • What objectives influenced your questions?
  • How many users did you interview?
  • What were the main insights from the interviews?
  • How did you use your findings?

Personas

We wanted to form a deeper understanding of our users' goals, needs, experiences, and behaviors. So, we created 4 personas for each of our user segments. They were based on user interviews and surveys, and we kept updating them throughout the project as we gathered more data. We used these personas whenever we wanted to step out of ourselves and reconsider our initial ideas.

User Journey

With the business goals in mind, we made sure that our users reach the checkout screen without any hiccups. So, we sketched a current-state user journey map to identify opportunities for improvement. We identified 3 representative steps and potential drop-off points in the flow. By understanding user behavior throughout the entire design process, we were able to confirm which steps were going smoothly and which ones were too complicated to conversion rates.

  • How and why did you choose which journey to map?
  • How did you test and validate the map?
  • What did the journey mapping reveal?
  • What did you do with the validated journey?
  • What changes in the design due to user journey mapping?

Card Sorting

To make sure that the work information architecture is aligned with user expectations, we had a remote card sorting session using an online tool like Optimal Workshop. Our goal was to find simple, intuitive language for the categories and information that end users would find easy to categories with 11 participants in each.

  • What did you want to achieve with card sorting?
  • What type of card sorting method did you work with?
  • How many participants did you recruit?
  • How did you interpret the results?
  • How did the new structure improve the design?

Sketches

Moving forward to the ideation process with low-fidelity sketches and wireframes to accelerate decision-making through visualizations without using time. My sketches were based on insights from our research and brainstorming. They were focused on the fact that there were too many options listed in the flow. We came across the findings that focused on entire design process to make sure that our app have slots of our primary goals and ideas.

  • What was the main purpose of your sketches? (e.g., preparing testing, brainstorming?)
  • How did they help you work a better-working tool?
  • If you made multiple versions what were their main differences?
  • How did the sketches throughout the entire design process?
  • Explain the layout and arrangement of the elements.
  • How did your sketches help you move forward?

Wireframes

Using Figma, I translated my first sketches into low-fidelity wireframes. Then, I worked with the development team to test the basic user flow concept for building content-based on. We made many wireframes and shared each for iterative feedback. Finally, we created a few alternatives and moved on to meaning and more detailed wireframes.

  • Did you create high- or low-fidelity wireframes?
  • Did you iterate on a few different versions?
  • Did you test with the wireframes? What were your findings?
  • How many iterations did you iterate?

Usability Testing

Finally, before we focused usability of the existing solution is a growing problem. So we focused on user experience. My plan was to identify and fix significant issues. The plan were to get more eyes and focus on a heuristic evaluation. I also prioritized based on what feedback comes after the usability testing phase. We conducted heuristic evaluation and learned a lot from the test participants.

  • What objectives influenced your task?
  • How did you develop your process script and tasks?
  • How was your testing experience process?
  • What did you find issues?
  • How did the findings inform your next iterations?
